Time

Jan 15th, 2013

If the way we spend our time is the surest indication of who we are, then my calling in life must be fruit fly genocide. Now, I’m no sadist. I’m not evil. My hat is whiter than my ancestors’ preferred subterranean source of dietary starch. Whatever I’m doing, though, the pests always make their way over for some target practice, and I oblige with canny ruthlessness.

Recently I had to make the transition from working full-time over winter break to working less than half-time as school has started up again. Since that change, my working time has been increasingly besieged by meetings and other such mundane affairs. There’s nothing wrong with them in themselves, but they have a way of grinding up my time into crumbs when what I need are loaves. Doubling the amount of time I can continuously work on something (within reason) tends to increase my productivity by a factor considerably higher than two.

What with everything else I have going on in my life right now, it can be tempting to resign myself to the tasks that more resemble flyswatting than matters of actual significance (which, unfortunately for my metaphor, can include actually resolving bugs). Satsifying though it may be to see the entrails of small, bothersome insects splayed across my palms, the figurative fruit flies that life sends my way can rarely be dispatched with such abandon. The things I consider to be tedious annoyances may well be perceived differently by others, and it would do me well to find the good in these things before writing them off as time forever lost.

Or something like that.
